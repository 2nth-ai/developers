How we build

Platform principles

01

Skills as atoms

Every capability is a versioned, documented skill. Skills compose into workflows. Workflows become products. Products serve customers.

02

Tokens as currency

One unit of work across all services — AI inference, builds, infrastructure. Priced in ZAR. No surprise bills. You control spend.

03

Open docs, metered execution

Skill documentation is CC BY-SA. Read, learn, fork freely. Execution through the platform is token-metered. Knowledge is free, compute isn't.

04

Partners own their customers

Your brand, your domain, your customer relationships. 2nth.ai powers the infrastructure. Revenue share, not revenue capture.

05

Africa-first, globally useful

Built in South Africa. Priced in ZAR. POPIA-compliant. Data residency in Africa-South-1. But the skills and agents work anywhere.

06

Human in the loop

AI proposes, humans approve. Every critical decision has a review gate. Agents work autonomously on tasks, escalate on judgement calls.

Why here, why now

Built in Africa for the world

The opportunity

AI is creating a new category of software company — one where a small team with the right skills can build what used to take 50 engineers. But the tools assume you're in San Francisco with a USD credit card.

2nth.ai is built for the rest of the world. Priced in ZAR. Deployed on infrastructure with African data residency. POPIA-compliant by architecture. And designed so that a business owner in Durban has the same capabilities as a funded startup in the Bay Area.

The model

We don't build products for clients. We build the platform that lets partners build products for their clients. Every partner who joins adds domain expertise to the skill tree. Every skill they contribute makes the platform more valuable for the next partner.

This is a network effect — but for capability, not just users. The more skills in the tree, the more powerful every agent becomes. And because skills resolve dependencies automatically, a new partner inherits everything that came before them.
