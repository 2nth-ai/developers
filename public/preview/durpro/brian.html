We want to dismiss an employee for poor performance. What's the correct process?
Poor performance dismissal follows the LRA Schedule 8 — Code of Good Practice: Dismissal, specifically items 8 and 9.
REQUIRED STEPS: 1. ASSESS — Is the performance standard reasonable? Were they trained? Do they have the tools? (Item 8(1)) 2. COUNSEL — Inform employee of the performance gap. Set clear targets + timeline. Document the conversation. 3. SUPPORT — Provide training, mentoring, or assistance. Give reasonable time to improve (typically 4-12 weeks). 4. REVIEW — Measure against the agreed targets. Document results. If still below standard, proceed. 5. HEARING — Formal incapacity hearing (not misconduct). Employee may bring a representative (s18(2) LRA). Consider: transfer, demotion, further training. 6. DECISION — If no viable alternative, dismiss with notice per BCEA s37 (1 week per year of service, minimum 1 week, maximum 4 weeks). REFERENCES: • LRA Schedule 8, Items 8-9 (Incapacity: Poor Performance) • BCEA s37 (Notice periods) • LRA s188 (Fair reason + fair procedure required)
⚠ Critical: Poor performance is incapacity, not misconduct. Do NOT use a disciplinary hearing format. The process must be supportive and developmental before becoming dismissive. Getting this wrong is the #1 reason employers lose at the CCMA.
What about a TES employee who's been on site for 4 months?
Section 198A kicks in here. This is critical for DurPro's TES operations:
SECTION 198A — DEEMED EMPLOYMENT After 3 months of continuous service, a TES employee earning below the BCEA threshold (currently R254,371.67/yr) is DEEMED to be the employee of the CLIENT, not the TES. YOUR SITUATION: Employee: 4 months on site = past the 3-month trigger If earning below threshold → deemed client employee IMPLICATIONS FOR THE CLIENT: • Client is now the employer for unfair dismissal purposes • Client must comply with s197 if restructuring • TES remains employer for: - Payment of wages - Statutory deductions (UIF, SDL, PAYE) - Administrative compliance WHAT DURPRO ADVISES: 1. Check if employee earns above/below threshold 2. If below: inform client of deemed employment status 3. Ensure client's policies apply to the employee 4. Document the arrangement clearly 5. Don't terminate to "reset the clock" — this is specifically prohibited (s198A(5)) REFERENCE: LRA s198A (inserted by Labour Relations Amendment Act 6 of 2014, effective 1 January 2015)
Draft a basic fixed-term contract clause for seasonal packhouse work

FIXED-TERM CONTRACT — SEASONAL WORK CLAUSE 1. NATURE OF EMPLOYMENT 1.1 The Employee is employed on a fixed-term basis as defined in Section 198B of the LRA. 1.2 This contract is for the duration of the [citrus/mango] packing season, commencing on [DATE] and terminating on [DATE] or upon completion of the season's packing operations, whichever occurs first. 2. JUSTIFICATION (s198B(3)) The fixed-term nature of this contract is justified by the seasonal nature of the work — packing operations are dependent on harvest timing and cannot be performed outside of the packing season. 3. EQUAL TREATMENT (s198B(8)) The Employee shall not be treated less favourably than comparable permanent employees unless there is a justifiable reason for different treatment. 4. RENEWAL EXPECTATION (s198B(5)) The Employee may reasonably expect renewal if: - They are employed for longer than 3 months; AND - The employer does not give a justifiable reason for non-renewal. In such case, non-renewal is treated as a dismissal and must be procedurally and substantively fair. 5. NOTICE Per BCEA s37: 1 week notice if employed less than 6 months; 2 weeks if employed 6 months to 1 year. ⚠ NOTE: If the employee has been employed on successive fixed-term contracts for the same seasonal work for more than 2 consecutive seasons, consider whether permanent employment with seasonal layoff (s189) is more appropriate. The CCMA is increasingly skeptical of rolling seasonal fixed-terms.
