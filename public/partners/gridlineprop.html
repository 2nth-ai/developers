The compliance framework

South African construction is governed by three interlocking regulatory bodies. GridLineProp's 35 years of practical experience with all three has been encoded into the biz/property/za skill domain.

SA

SANS 10400

The technical code. Defines how structures must be designed and built. 23 parts from foundations to energy.

PlansStructuralFireEnergy
CI

CIDB

The contractor registry. Defines who is eligible to build. Grades 1–9 with tender value limits.

GradingTendersProcurement
NH

NHBRC

Consumer protection. Defines what warranties and inspections apply to residential construction.

RegistrationInspectionsWarranties

Skills contributed

GridLineProp's domain expertise powers four production skills in the biz/property/za tree. Each skill encodes decades of real project experience into structured, reusable intelligence.

SANS 10400 — National Building Regulations

Production

All 23 parts of the SA building code. Plan submission requirements, occupancy classifications (A1–J4), competent person requirements, energy compliance routes, and deemed-to-satisfy vs rational design guidance.

CIDB — Contractor Registration & Grading

Production

Grades 1–9 with tender value limits, 10 classes of works (CE, GB, ME, etc.), registration and upgrade process, financial sponsorship rules, procurement via the Standard for Uniformity, and the BUILD programme.

Production

Builder registration process, home enrolment (15-day rule), 4-stage inspection framework, 3-tier warranty structure (3-month/1-year/5-year), complaints and conciliation process.

Expertise × skills map

How GridLineProp's real-world experience maps to each skill's content:

Plan submission

Energy (XA)
OFF-GRIDz advisory division — solar, energy-efficient envelope design, and reduced municipal dependency built into every home as standard, not as an afterthought.
Water systems
Greywater recycling, borehole, and rainwater harvesting systems designed into builds. Maps to SANS 10400 Parts P (drainage) and R (stormwater).
Contractors
29 years of vetting, grading, and developing contractors. Known for staff and subcontractor development — the CIDB BUILD programme philosophy in practice.
Inspections
